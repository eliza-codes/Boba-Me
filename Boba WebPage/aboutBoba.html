About Boba


Thank you for choosing Boba Me to get your facts about your favorite drink, boba. This article has been written in 2019 so some facts might not be up to date.


Your favorite drink is often referred to as boba. Did you know, boba is actually the small tapioca pearls in your drink. These pearls have a chewy texture when cooked correctly. It may be slightly sweet depending on which sweeteners used. Pearls normally contains up to 63 calories. And with a total of 15 grams of these pearls, there will be plenty of carbs, preservatives, flavoring and coloring. Your drink can have various of add-ons like, lychee coconut or fruit slices.

Boba is sweet and you will want to drink more but keep in mind, there will be little health benefits. Boba is sweet because it is loaded with added sugar and flavor. Think of soda, there are similar health consequences. They are both loaded with sugar so it will raise blood sugar levels and risks for diabetes and/or future heart problems.

Basic Health Facts

From a recent research by the US Department of Agriculture, an 8-ounce serving can contain at least 120 calories and 2 grams of fat. This kind of fat is not non-saturated, as mentioned earlier, there are risks of diabetes and future heart problems. You are also raising your cholesterol levels.

Where does the fat come from? Fat is not commonly found in boba but you can in milk teas, you will find fat in the milk used. Depending on the drink you order, you could also find fat inside the cream used. Although this is highly unlikely because shops use non-dairy creams.
